Overview

Enhancing Construction Planning with BIM Models

This project investigated the ways in which BIM models can enhance planning, logistics, and scheduling in construction projects by integrating them with the Pilarr Project Production Management (PPM) platform.

My Contribution

As a member of the R&D team, I contributed to the development of Revit models featuring structured parameters intended for interaction with Pilarr's planning environment. The objective was to link design data through BIM parameters with production management, facilitating improved collaboration among architects, engineers, and on-site teams.

Process

The process included establishing consistent data structures for quantities and materials, which could subsequently be tied to digital work packages and construction schedules. This framework promoted more predictable planning and resource allocation, while also enabling insights from one project to benefit future initiatives.

Outcome

Through this collaborative effort, I gained practical experience in digital construction workflows, model-based coordination, and the expansive potential of BIM as a live data source for project management. This research illustrated how the integration of design and production data can enhance transparency, minimise variability, and lead to more efficient and predictable construction processes.
